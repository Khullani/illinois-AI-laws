⚡ Why This Matters Right Now
PA 103-0804 took effect January 1, 2026. Every Illinois employer using AI in any hiring, promotion, or discipline decision must have transparency notices deployed and disparate-impact-free systems — today. The IDHR is finalizing enforcement rules that will trigger investigations and complaints. Meanwhile, 29 more bills are moving through committee with an April 24 deadline — the regulatory surface area is about to expand dramatically.

Employer Readiness
PA 103-0804 · PA 101-0260 — AI Employment Laws
Not assessed▼
PA 103-0804Effective Jan 1, 2026
Have you issued a formal AI transparency notice to all employees and applicants explaining which employment decisions use AI?
Required whenever AI influences employment decisions — regardless of whether discrimination occurs. Failure to notify is itself an IHRA violation.
You are currently in violation. IDHR can accept complaints today. Any employee or applicant can file a charge. Priority: inventory all AI-assisted decisions and issue notices immediately.
PA 103-0804
Have you audited your AI hiring/promotion tools for disparate impact on protected classes?
The statute prohibits AI in employment decisions where it results in discrimination — even unintentional. Resume screeners, assessments, and interview analyzers all fall in scope.
Highest-exposure item. Unaudited AI tools create liability for every employment decision they influence. Engage counsel for a privileged bias audit and send due diligence questionnaires to all AI vendors.
PA 103-0804
Have you verified that no AI tool uses zip codes as a proxy for protected characteristics?
Zip code proxy targeting is explicitly banned — not a gray area. Common in off-the-shelf recruiting tools.
Enumerated violation. Many off-the-shelf tools use geographic data that creates this liability. You need a technical review of every input feature in every AI tool.
PA 103-0804
Have you updated your employee handbook and HR policies to reflect AI anti-discrimination requirements?
Documentation gap. Outdated handbooks weaken your position in any IDHR investigation. Update by end of Q2 2026 at latest.
PA 101-0260In effect since 2020
If you use AI-analyzed video interviews, do you provide written explanation and obtain consent before AI evaluation?
Six years of exposure. This law has been in effect since 2020 and is frequently overlooked. If your video interview vendor uses AI analysis, you need documented proof of notice + consent for every candidate.
PA 103-0804
Have you trained HR staff, hiring managers, and recruiters on the new AI employment law requirements?
People risk. Untrained managers making AI-assisted employment decisions are your most likely source of a compliance failure.

Healthcare / Mental Health
PA 104-0539 (WOPR Act) — Effective Aug 2025
Not assessed▼
PA 104-0539
Have you audited all clinical AI tools to ensure none can function as an independent therapist?
The WOPR Act flatly prohibits AI from serving as an independent mental health therapist — chatbots, triage systems, and symptom checkers included.
Active violation. Any AI tool interpretable as independent therapy is noncompliant right now. Audit immediately — restructure or remove.
PA 104-0539
Have you disabled all emotion detection / sentiment analysis in clinical-facing AI tools?
Flat prohibition — not a disclosure requirement.
Absolute prohibition. Many telehealth platforms include emotion detection by default. You need a technical inventory of every AI feature in every vendor product touching clinical workflows.
PA 104-0539
Do you have a documented Human Oversight Protocol for all AI-assisted clinical decisions?
Missing infrastructure. Without documented clinician review, your organization lacks the compliance framework to defend its practices.

Not assessed▼
PA 104-0399
Does your district have a formal AI Acceptable Use Policy for staff and students?
ISBE guidance is coming. Get ahead with a draft policy now rather than scrambling when ISBE publishes — you'll need board approval time.
PA 104-0399
Have you begun developing AI literacy curriculum, including deepfake recognition?
Curriculum mandate. Curriculum development takes semesters, not weeks. Start now.
PA 104-0399
Do you have a formal review process before any AI tool is deployed in classrooms?
Shadow AI risk. Teachers and students are already using AI tools without district knowledge. Unvetted tools create simultaneous FERPA and state AI law exposure.

Content, Media & IP
PA 103-0882 · PA 103-0880 · PA 103-0806
Not assessed▼
PA 103-0882/0880
Do all talent/performer contracts include specific descriptions of intended AI digital replica use, with legal representation confirmed?
Contracts without "reasonably specific" use descriptions and legal representation are void — not voidable, void.
Void contracts. Any AI-generated content based on a noncompliant contract has no legal foundation. Audit all talent agreements immediately.
PA 103-0880
Have you audited existing AI-generated content for unauthorized use of any person's voice, image, or likeness?
Right of Publicity violation. In effect since August 2024. Every piece of AI-generated content in your library needs a consent audit.
PA 103-0806
Do you have content moderation policies specifically addressing AI-generated nonconsensual intimate imagery?
Criminal statute. Not civil — criminal penalties. If you host user-generated content, you need explicit moderation policies and reporting mechanisms.

05What's Coming Next

29 pending bills. Two at 2nd reading. Organizations that wait for enactment to start planning will be behind on day one.

⚡
HB 3021 — Consumer AI Deception
2nd Reading · Highest momentum
▼
Would prohibit chatbots/AI agents from misleading consumers into believing they're human. Every customer-facing chatbot needs disclosure.
🔮 Prepare now
  • Audit all customer-facing chatbots — do they self-identify as AI?
  • Design disclosure UX patterns ready to deploy on enactment
⚡
HB 4980 — Meaningful Human Control
2nd Reading · Public employers
▼
Would prohibit public employers from using automated decisions without human review. Impact assessments required. Private right of action.
🔮 Prepare now
  • Government agencies: inventory all automated decision systems
  • Draft impact assessment templates
  • Build human review workflows for decisions currently lacking them
⚡
SB 3262 / SB 3384 — Companion AI Safety
First-of-its-kind · High political salience
▼
Would regulate emotionally adaptive AI companions — crisis intervention, persistent disclosure, parental consent, self-harm detection.
🔮 Prepare now
  • Build crisis detection into product roadmaps
  • Implement age verification and parental consent flows
⚡
SB 3364 — Algorithmic Civil Rights Expansion
Beyond employment → housing, credit, public accommodations
▼
Would amend Illinois Human Rights Act to cover AI in real estate, lending, credit, and public accommodations.
🔮 Prepare now
  • Financial services: audit AI lending/credit models for disparate impact
  • Real estate: review automated valuation models
  • Retail/hospitality: evaluate AI affecting service, pricing, or access
